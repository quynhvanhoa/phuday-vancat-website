1. History

Phủ Dầy Vân Cát is located in Kim Thái Commune, Vụ Bản District, Ninh Bình Province (formerly part of Nam Định Province). It is one of the three principal temples of the Phủ Dầy complex — together with Phủ Tiên Hương and Phủ Quảng Cung — forming the leading centre of Mother Goddess worship in the Red River Delta.

According to historical records and folk legend, Phủ Vân Cát was built on the very site where Holy Mother Liễu Hạnh descended for the second time — a sacred event preserved in folk memory through the centuries. Through successive dynasties, the temple has been restored and expanded many times, yet it has retained its ancient grandeur and original spiritual essence.

2. Legend of Mother Liễu Hạnh — The Second Incarnation

Mother Liễu Hạnh — one of the "Four Immortals" of Vietnamese folk belief — was originally the celestial maiden Princess Quỳnh Hoa, daughter of the Jade Emperor. According to legend, she descended to the human world three times to help the people and propagate moral teachings.

Her second incarnation took place in Vân Cát village, an event preserved in folk memory through countless generations. In this form, she embodied the highest virtues: filial piety to her parents, faithfulness to her husband, love for her children, and compassion for all beings. The image of the Holy Mother has become the symbol of the Vietnamese woman — at once gentle and strong, pure and powerful.

3. Architectural Heritage

Phủ Dầy Vân Cát bears the unmistakable stamp of traditional Northern Vietnamese temple architecture. The complex includes:

  • Three-Entrance Gate (Tam Quan): a majestic entrance with curved roofs and ornamented eaves.
  • Front Hall (Tiền Tế): a solemn space for offerings.
  • Middle Hall (Trung Từ): where the main rituals are performed.
  • Inner Sanctum (Hậu Cung): the most sacred space, housing the statue of the Holy Mother.
  • Pavilions of the Dames and Princes (Lầu Cô — Lầu Cậu): dedicated to attendant deities.

The wood carvings, horizontal lacquered boards, parallel sentences, and dragon and phoenix motifs all represent the pinnacle of traditional Vietnamese sculptural art. The system of Hán–Nôm horizontal boards and parallel sentences is a precious archive of the culture, literature, and spiritual beliefs of past generations.

4. Official Recognitions

The Phủ Dầy complex, to which Phủ Dầy Vân Cát belongs, has been honoured at multiple levels:

  • National Architectural Heritage Site — recognising the outstanding architectural value of the complex.
  • Practices Related to the Việt Beliefs in the Mother Goddesses of Three Realms — inscribed on UNESCO's Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity in 2016.
  • Phủ Dầy Festival — recognised as a National Intangible Cultural Heritage.

These distinctions affirm Phủ Dầy Vân Cát's place not only as a spiritual landmark but also as a cultural symbol of the Vietnamese nation.
