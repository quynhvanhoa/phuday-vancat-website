The grandest feast of the year, commemorating the Mother's transcendence. The Phủ Dầy festival runs solemnly from the 1st to the 10th day of the third lunar month, with many traditional rituals.

The March Phủ Dầy Festival

A National Intangible Cultural Heritage

Festival Programme

The Phủ Dầy festival is one of the largest spiritual festivals in northern Vietnam, held from the 1st to the 10th day of the third lunar month each year. It gathers tens of thousands of pilgrims and visitors who come to offer incense and pay homage to the Holy Mother.

Principal Rituals

  • Procession of the Holy Mother: a solemn procession through the main temples of the complex.
  • Character-Forming Ceremony (Kéo Chữ): a unique tradition in which participants form auspicious Chinese characters with their bodies.
  • Chầu Văn Singing Contest: bringing together gifted singers and mediums.
  • Lantern Festival: held at night, creating a magical and sacred atmosphere.

Visitor Guidance

Visitors are encouraged to arrive early and to dress in modest, respectful attire. Offerings may be simple (flowers, fruits, incense) or simply a sincere heart. Temple staff are always on hand to guide visitors through the rituals.
