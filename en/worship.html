Overview of Mother Goddess Worship

The veneration of the Mother Goddess (Đạo Mẫu) is one of the oldest and most distinctive folk traditions of the Vietnamese people. At its heart is the honouring of the Mother — the symbol of birth, nurturing, and protection. The Three Realms — Four Palaces tradition reflects the Vietnamese conception of the cosmos, of nature, and of humanity's place within it.

On 1 December 2016, UNESCO officially inscribed "Practices Related to the Việt Beliefs in the Mother Goddesses of Three Realms" on its Representative List of the Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity.

The Four Palaces

The spiritual world of the Mother Goddess tradition is divided into four realms (Tứ Phủ), each governed by a Mother Goddess and associated with a distinct colour:

Among them, Mother Liễu Hạnh is revered as the principal Mother — the embodiment of the essence of all Four Palaces. Phủ Dầy Vân Cát is one of the most important centres of her worship.

The Hierarchy of Deities

Below the Mother Goddesses extends a rich pantheon of deities, organised into ranks:

  • The Five Great Mandarins — overseeing the realms on behalf of the Mothers.
  • The Dames of the Four Palaces (Chầu Bà) — usually goddesses of historical merit.
  • The Princes of the Four Palaces (Ông Hoàng) — often deified historical figures or generals.
  • The Holy Maidens (Thánh Cô) — attendants of the Mothers, linked with deeply human and sacred stories.
  • The Holy Lads (Thánh Cậu) — youthful spirits, at once playful and dignified.

The Lên Đồng (Spirit Mediumship) Ritual

Lên đồng — also known as hầu đồng or hầu bóng — is the central ritual of the Mother Goddess tradition. The spirit medium, dressed in elaborate costume and accompanied by music and dance, "invites" the deities to descend and bestow blessings upon the faithful.

A typical session consists of several "incarnations" (giá), each devoted to a particular deity. The medium changes costumes, offers incense, performs ritual dances (with swords, fans, or silk scarves depending on the deity), delivers oracles, and distributes blessings.

Chầu Văn Singing

Chầu văn is the sacred musical genre developed specifically for the lên đồng ritual. The chants narrate the legends and praise the virtues of the deities, guiding the progression of each incarnation. Chầu văn combines vocal poetry with the moon-shaped lute, drums, and percussion — creating an atmosphere both transcendent and intoxicating.

Cultural Values

The Mother Goddess tradition embodies a confluence of values:

  • Humanistic value: honouring motherhood, elevating love and protection.
  • Artistic value: crystallised in costume, music, dance, and architecture.
  • Historical value: reflecting history through the figures it venerates.
  • Communal value: binding people together, preserving cultural identity.
